How Scores Work

Full methodology — five pillars, transparent formulas, auditable weights

📐 The KRANK Score — Overview
Every KRANK score is a single 0–100 number built from five independently scored pillars. Each pillar is first normalised to a 0–100 scale, then combined using the weights below. Media deliberately carries the largest weight so that celebrities with high press impact and public visibility are not disadvantaged by low social posting activity. All inputs are log-scaled where follower counts are involved to prevent mega-star audiences from overwhelming mid-tier celebrities.
Final Formula
FIVE PILLARS · EXACT WEIGHTS
KRANK Score =
  (Media Score × 0.30) +
  (Social Score × 0.25) +
  (Brand Power Score × 0.20) +
  (Fan Activity Score × 0.15) +
  (Search Trend Score × 0.10)
When social data is unavailable for a celebrity, the 25% social weight is redistributed proportionally across the remaining four pillars so the final score is not artificially suppressed.
1 · Media Score
WEIGHT: 30% OF FINAL SCORE
Media Score = (Media Volume × 0.50) + (Media Quality × 0.30) + (Media Recency × 0.20)
SUBSECTIONWEIGHTDEFINITION
Media Volume50%Article and mention count over a rolling 30-day window, normalised 0–100. Captures total press mass regardless of outlet quality.
Media Quality30%Weighted by outlet authority — Tier 1 (major TV, national newspaper, top magazine) scores higher than Tier 3 (blog, small niche site). Raw count is authority-weighted before normalisation.
Media Recency20%Rewards current relevance. Mentions in the most recent 7 days decay slower than older mentions. Celebrities with fresh press score higher than those with similar historic volume but no recent coverage.
2 · Social Score
WEIGHT: 25% OF FINAL SCORE
Social Score = (Social Reach Score × 0.70) + (Social Engagement Score × 0.30)

Social Reach Score = Σ [ log₁₀(platform_audience + 1) × platform_weight ] ÷ log₁₀(max_audience + 1) × 100

Platform weights — YouTube: 40% · Instagram: 35% · TikTok: 25%
SUBSECTIONWEIGHTDEFINITION
Social Reach70%Log-scaled audience across Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube using platform-specific commercial weights. Log scaling prevents a celebrity with 100M followers from scoring 10× a celebrity with 10M — logarithmic compression models real-world diminishing marginal reach.
Social Engagement30%Recent interaction rate (likes + comments + shares ÷ recent followers), normalised 0–100. Rewards active, engaged audiences. A celebrity with 1M highly engaged followers can outperform one with 20M passive followers on this subscore.
YouTube carries the highest platform weight because it commands premium CPM rates, has the longest content shelf-life, and is the strongest signal for English-language cross-cultural commercial reach. Instagram is weighted second for brand-deal and luxury market relevance. TikTok is weighted third — strong for viral discovery, but audience monetisation and commercial deal value are currently lower per follower.
3 · Brand Power Score
WEIGHT: 20% OF FINAL SCORE
Brand Power Score = (Active Endorsements × 0.40) + (Brand Prestige × 0.30) + (Est. Commercial Value × 0.30)
SUBSECTIONWEIGHTDEFINITION
Active Endorsements40%Count of currently active brand partnerships, normalised against the benchmark maximum of 20 simultaneous deals = 100. Includes beauty, fashion, electronics, food, finance, and lifestyle categories.
Brand Prestige30%Quality-weighted score based on partner brand tier. Luxury / global tier-1 brands (CHANEL, Dior, Louis Vuitton) score highest. Mass-market and regional brands score lower. One luxury deal can outweigh several mid-tier deals.
Est. Commercial Value30%Analyst estimate of total annual endorsement value, normalised 0–100. Combines deal count × estimated per-deal fee based on category benchmarks. Captures both volume and rate-per-partnership.
4 · Fan Activity Score
WEIGHT: 15% OF FINAL SCORE
Fan Activity Score = (Community Activity × 0.40) + (Direct Fan Signals × 0.35) + (Event / Match-Day Activity × 0.25)
SUBSECTIONWEIGHTDEFINITION
Community Activity40%Fan-post volume, hashtag activity, and fan community engagement across fancafes, Reddit, Twitter/X fan accounts, and Discord servers. Measures the ambient energy of a celebrity's fanbase.
Direct Fan Signals35%Comment intensity on celebrity-owned posts, livestream chat volume, Bubble / Weverse message activity, fan voting participation, and streaming chart spikes driven by organised fan action.
Event / Match-Day Activity25%Concert, tour, or match-day spikes in online activity. For athletes, includes gameday engagement across social and fan sites. For musicians, includes ticketing demand, venue sell-out data, and post-concert fan activity surges.
5 · Search Trend Score
WEIGHT: 10% OF FINAL SCORE
Search Trend Score = (Google Trends Strength × 0.70) + (Search Consistency × 0.30)
SUBSECTIONWEIGHTDEFINITION
Google Trends Strength70%Peak search volume index over the rolling 30-day period, normalised against the highest-scoring celebrity. Captures viral moments, comeback surges, and news-driven search spikes.
Search Consistency30%Standard deviation-based consistency score over 90 days. Celebrities with sustained search interest score higher than one-hit wonders with a single viral moment. Rewards long-term relevance.
Normalisation Rules
HOW INPUTS BECOME 0–100 SCORES
INPUT TYPEMETHOD
Follower countsLog₁₀ transformation, then min-max normalised against the dataset maximum. Formula: log₁₀(n+1) ÷ log₁₀(max+1) × 100. Prevents mega-audiences from dominating.
Count-based inputsBenchmarked against a defined ceiling (e.g., 20 brand deals = 100). Capped at 100 to prevent extreme outliers skewing the dataset.
Rate-based inputsAlready expressed as percentages or rates. Min-max normalised within the dataset before weighting.
Missing dataWhen social platform data is unavailable, the 25% Social Score weight is redistributed proportionally across the remaining pillars rather than scoring as zero. Minimum data confidence thresholds apply.
Data Confidence
TRANSPARENCY STANDARD
Each celebrity entry carries a Data Confidence rating (High / Medium / Low) reflecting how many of the five pillars have verified primary-source data vs. analyst estimates. High confidence = 4–5 pillars verified. Medium = 2–3 pillars. Low = 1 pillar or heavy estimation. Scores from Low-confidence entries should be treated as directional, not definitive.
